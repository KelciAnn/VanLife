#VAN LIFE

The destinations to go in a 'destinationless' living

Our team has pushed the envelope on travel! Everyone of us fully emersed ourselves in the van lifestyle. Focusing on what we loved and moving towards it with blind (but logical) desire. Once we'd all traveled enough to understand the pros and cons of our lifestyle and the rewards we felt from having the freedom we did, a business idea was spawned. We have dedicated ourselves to helping those who are searching for the freedom we found.

The Truths

Cons

The Stigma:

The first thing to address about living in a van is the old stigma that is still somewhat attached to it. Nowadays (for younger travlers) living in a van is more representative of being free and living a minamlist lifestyle. However, not everyone thinks that way. "Living in a van down by the river" is a common quote used to describe becoming a failure in life. When living in a van expect some people not to overly understand you choices.


Showering:

One of the biggest problems with living in a van full-time is showering. In a van, it is likely that you will not have a shower. Some vans do have portable showers, but not all. In some areas, showers are easy to find but others are not. Wet wipes have become a staple item to have in the van.


The toilet and finding a bathroom:

Aside from showers, there is the whole toilet issue! There are small portable toilets. They can be a hassle to use and most people only carry them for self-contained certification or in case of an EXTREME emergency. That means you need to always have a public toliet in your mind.


Pros

Saving Money:

When you live in a van your costs are minimal. No rent and no electricity or water bills. Your main cost will be fuel (gas) and food. It is a very cheap way to live or travel! For those on a budget, van life is perfect!
In fact, while I lived in my van we spent around $60 to $100 a day between myself and my partner. Typically staying upto 3 nights at every place. Saving on gas and food.


Minimalism:

In this day and age, the idea of living a minamlistic lifestyle is glamourized. Similar to the trend of "Tiny Houses", when you live in a van you are living very minimally. Reducing the amount of possessions you have.

Freedom:

Who is not searching for some form of freedom? Freedom from the restraints of a 9-5. Freedom from social media. Freedom from a typical life. Who knows what kind of freedom you're searching for. Only YOU can find this. However, we are always here to help you along the way. Go out, find it, experience it, love it!
